Recipe: Spring Pasta Salad With Peas and Pesto

written Jul 16th, 2014 in food

Here is a great recipe for spring/summer. It uses fresh and light ingredients to add some nice flavors to a classic pesto pasta recipe.

Ingredients

For the pesto

  • 1 bunch (approx 1 cup) fresh basil
  • 1 large garlic clove, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons chopped toasted walnuts or pinenuts
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • ½ cup grated parmesan cheese
  • Olive oil

If you want to spice it up a little bit, you can also add

  • A few sprigs of mint or parsley
  • 1 ripe avocado

Combine the herbs, garlic, nuts, lemon juice and cheese in a food processor. Blend it and slowly pour in some oil. How much oil is up to you, depending on how thick you want your pesto to be. I went with 3-4 tablespoons. Add salt and black pepper to taste.

For the salad

  • 300g short pasta
  • 1 can(150g) green peas
  • 200g cherry tomatoes, halved

Heat up the green peas and cook the pasta in boiling water until al dente. Drain both when ready. Mix in the pesto with the still hot pasta to release the flavors from the basil and garlic. Then mix in the peas and the tomatoes. You can serve it hot or put it in the fridge to cool.
